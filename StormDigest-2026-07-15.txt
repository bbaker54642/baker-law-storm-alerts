STORM: South/Central Texas flash flooding — Brazoria, Jackson, and Matagorda counties, July 11–15, 2026
---
People in Brazoria, Jackson, and Matagorda counties are assessing flooding damage from the storm system that soaked Central and South Texas starting July 11. [1] Governor Abbott issued a state disaster declaration for 59 counties on July 14, including Brazoria County. [2]

The first thing many people discover after a flooding event is that their homeowners policy does not cover it. Standard homeowners policies exclude water damage that enters from the ground up. The policy covers wind. It does not cover what floodwater did to walls, floors, and structural materials it reached.

People who carry separate flood policies through the National Flood Insurance Program can file a claim. [3] NFIP building coverage caps at $250,000 for residential structures. Contents coverage is separate and caps at $100,000. Rebuilding often costs more than those limits allow. [3]

For TWIA policyholders along the coast, the question shifts to which policy covers which damage. TWIA covers wind and hail. Flood damage falls under a separate flood policy, if one exists at all. Adjusters will argue about whether wind or water caused each element of the loss. That argument determines who pays, how much, and when.

I have worked these split claims after coastal flooding events. The two coverage types do not add up to a full rebuild in most cases. The policies in force on the date of the event determine what the carrier owes.

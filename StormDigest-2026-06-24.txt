STORM: Tropical Storm Arthur - Matagorda County, Brazoria County, and Coastal Texas
---
Tropical Storm Arthur made landfall near Matagorda County on June 17. [1] Flooding submerged homes in rural Brazoria County, and power outages spread across Galveston and coastal Texas. [2] People in these counties are now dealing with their insurance carriers.

Some are finding out for the first time that their policy contains a named-storm deductible. That deductible is calculated as a percentage of the insured value of the home, not a flat dollar amount. A two percent deductible on a $300,000 home is $6,000 before the carrier pays anything. Carriers are applying named-storm deductibles even in areas where Arthur had already weakened before reaching the property. [3]

Flood damage and wind damage are governed by different parts of a policy, or entirely different policies. Water that entered from storm surge or rainfall is not covered by a standard homeowners policy. Wind-driven rain that entered through a damaged roof is a different question. Carriers dispute the cause of loss because the answer determines who pays.

Governor Abbott declared a state of emergency for 101 Texas counties. [4] FEMA had not approved disaster assistance for Arthur-affected areas as of June 23. [5] Policyholders across these counties are navigating these claims without federal support.

I have represented policyholders after every major tropical system that has touched this coast. The named-storm deductible dispute, the cause-of-loss dispute, and the delayed inspection do not come one at a time. Carriers run all three at once.

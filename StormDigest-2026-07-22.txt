⚠️ CRITICAL WATCH FOR NEXT RUN — TROPICAL STORM BERTHA:
Bertha is forecast to make first landfall in Louisiana (Plaquemines Parish) Wednesday evening July 22, then drift westward along the Gulf Coast toward Texas by Thursday–Friday July 24–25. A Tropical Storm Watch is already in effect for parts of SE Texas including Galveston County. Galveston County is a qualifying coastal county. If Bertha retains tropical storm organization at any Texas landfall — even weakened — it qualifies immediately under the coastal county criterion. Brazoria, Matagorda, and Jackson counties are also in the projected path.

Expected post angle if qualifying: TWIA named-storm deductible (same structural issue as Tropical Storm Arthur, June 24 digest); TWIA vs. flood policy split when surge and rain are both present; cause-of-loss disputes between wind and flood damage. Sources to check on next run: weather.gov/hgx (NWS Houston/Galveston), twia.org for any active bulletins, KHOU and Fox26 Houston live coverage.

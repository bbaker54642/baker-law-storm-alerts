STORM: EF-1 Tornado / Straight-Line Winds - Victoria and Calhoun Counties, Texas
(NOTE: Storm occurred May 1, 2026 - outside the June 7-10 search window. State disaster declaration issued May 7, 2026. SBA disaster loan program remains active as of June 10, 2026, per Federal Register amendment of June 4. Draft included because this storm is within Kevin Baker's primary coastal service area and the claims process is ongoing.)

---
Victoria and Calhoun County policyholders are now in the thick of the claims process from the May 1 storms. [1]

That afternoon, an EF-1 tornado and straight-line winds up to 120 mph swept across the Victoria Crossroads. [2] The National Weather Service damage survey confirmed 133 structures damaged in Victoria County, with 44 receiving major damage or total destruction. [3]

Here is what I see after a storm like this hits coastal Texas. Carriers use the straight-line versus tornadic wind distinction against policyholders. Exclusions are written around wind classifications that shift losses into uncovered categories. Manufactured homes and older structures took some of the worst damage, and carriers apply pre-existing condition language and maintenance exclusions to reduce what they pay.

Governor Abbott added Victoria and Calhoun counties to the state disaster declaration following the storm. [4] The SBA then expanded disaster loan eligibility to multiple counties across the Crossroads region, a formal recognition of disaster-level damage. [5]

Structural damage confirmed by a National Weather Service survey is not a disputed fact. What carriers choose to pay on that damage is.

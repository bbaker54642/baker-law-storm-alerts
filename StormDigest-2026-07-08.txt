STORM: West-Central Texas severe thunderstorm / straight-line winds — Abilene and Ranger, July 5, 2026
---
People in Abilene and the Big Country are now filing property insurance claims after severe thunderstorms hit West-Central Texas on the evening of July 5, 2026. [1] Wind gusts reached 80 miles per hour. [2] Apartment buildings lost roof sections. A family's home became uninhabitable after the roof began caving in. [3] A business owner's building was completely destroyed. [4] A building collapsed in Ranger during the same storm system. [1]

Standard homeowners policies cover wind damage. The disputes start when the adjuster arrives. Carriers write estimates that attribute structural failure to pre-existing deterioration, maintenance neglect, or construction defects rather than the storm. A collapsed roof becomes a partial payment. A destroyed building becomes an argument about actual cash value versus replacement cost.

Actual cash value settlements deduct for depreciation. A 15-year-old roof that was intact before the storm gets discounted in the carrier's estimate. People receive checks that do not cover what rebuilding costs. Many policies carry replacement cost coverage, but carriers pay actual cash value first and withhold the depreciation until repairs are complete. People who cannot fund the repairs out of pocket get stuck in that gap.

An 80 mph wind gust that collapses buildings is not a maintenance problem. The policy language controls what the carrier owes.

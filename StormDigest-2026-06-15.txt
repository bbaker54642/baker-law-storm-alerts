STORM: Flash flooding - Central Texas and coastal Southeast Texas (June 14-15, 2026)
---
Flooding hit central Texas and the Gulf Coast starting June 14. [1] Galveston County is under a flood watch. [2] Water rescues are ongoing in Waco and Austin. [3]

Standard homeowners policies exclude flood damage. That exclusion is broad. It covers overflow from rivers and bayous, surface water runoff, and stormwater. Most of what people see rising in their streets and yards falls inside that exclusion.

If you carry a National Flood Insurance Program policy, you have a federal program with its own coverage schedule. NFIP adjusters move quickly but work from a narrow list of covered items. They will often pay contents, finished floors, and elevated mechanicals at partial rates or exclude them entirely.

The harder claims arise when wind caused damage before the water arrived. A roof opened by wind prior to flooding is not the same legal situation as a structure that flooded without prior wind damage. Carriers and write-your-own NFIP adjusters both draw that line in their own favor.

Governor Abbott activated state emergency resources ahead of this event. [4] That activation often precedes a formal disaster declaration. When a declaration follows, it can open additional claim pathways that policyholders miss if they close out claims too quickly.

The documentation created in the first 72 hours is what adjusters work from. Insulation, wall cavities, and flooring do not stay visible long after floodwater recedes.

STORM: Severe microbursts / straight-line winds — Abilene and West-Central Texas, July 5, 2026
---
People in Abilene and across the Big Country are now filing wind damage claims. The National Weather Service confirmed that Sunday night's storms produced microbursts with gusts reaching 70 to 90 miles per hour across Fisher and Jones counties. [1] An 80-mile-per-hour gust was recorded near Abilene Christian University. [2] Structural damage to homes and businesses was reported across a wide area of West-Central Texas, and NWS damage surveys were still underway. [3]

I see this pattern play out with microburst claims. Carriers classify roof and structural components as depreciated and issue actual cash value payments even when a policy requires replacement cost coverage. Policyholders receive checks that do not cover what repairs actually cost.

Carriers also look to classify microburst damage as a maintenance issue. They argue the structure already had wear before the storm arrived, and that the storm was not the cause of the damage. That argument puts the burden of proof on the policyholder.

Texas law is clear on this. A replacement cost policy obligates the carrier to pay the full cost of restoring damaged property. Completing repairs triggers the release of withheld depreciation. Carriers know this obligation exists. The word maintenance does not make it go away.
